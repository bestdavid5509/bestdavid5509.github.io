Work

Indie RPG (in development)

A turn-based RPG built in Godot 4 with C# and .NET 9. Part passion project, part learning venture — designed to demonstrate game systems work, custom mechanics, and modern AI-assisted development workflows in a meaningful end-to-end project.

4-character party formation against an 8-enemy encounter.

The core mechanic is a timing-based input system: players coordinate parries, attacks, and abilities through closing-circle prompts that demand attention to timing rather than just menu selection. Standard, slow, and bouncing prompt variants create different attack textures across the combat system.

Timing prompt during a melee attack sequence.

Magic spells, multi-step attacks, and item usage extend the timing mechanic into more complex action sequences. Effects, animations, and music transitions are coordinated through a custom turn queue that handles camera work, sprite state, and gameplay logic in lockstep.

The Absorber character can learn enemy moves through perfect parries.

One character — the Absorber — can learn enemy moves through perfect parries, expanding the player's ability set through gameplay. A two-phase boss encounter against The Harbinger showcases this mechanic alongside dynamic music transitions and reveal animations.

Narrative dialogue with character speakers.

Technical highlights include camera zoom-clamp math that adapts to formation density (encounters scale from 1v1 up to 4v8), shader work for character state visualization, a content-offset system for sprite-frame positioning, and extensive use of AI-assisted development workflows (Claude Code) with rigorous review and verification at each step.

Built solo. All visual and audio assets are from human-created asset packs (Kenney, OpenGameArt, and various itch.io creators) — no AI-generated art or music. Full attribution provided in the project repository.
